Homie

Siberian Forest Cat


The Siberian cat is renowned for its remarkable jumping ability and possesses a robust and muscular physique. It boasts powerful hindquarters and large, rounded paws.


Due to the slightly longer hind legs compared to the front legs, the Siberian cat exhibits a gentle arch in its back.


The cat's fur consists of three distinct types: guard hair, awn hair, and down hair. Together, these layers form a dense, well-developed, semi-long to long triple-coat.


The water-repellent nature of the Siberian cat's coat serves as a protective shield against the harsh weather conditions experienced in Russia.



Sphynx Cat


The Sphynx cat is a unique and distinctive breed known for its lack of fur, which is more suitable for people with allergies


The most striking feature of the Sphynx cat is its naked skin, which can vary in color and pattern.


Some Sphynx cats may have a light covering of fuzz on certain parts of their body, such as the ears, tail, or nose.
It has a medium-sized build with a muscular body and a sturdy frame.


Due to their lack of fur, Sphynx cats require special care to maintain their skin health. Regular bathing is necessary to remove excess oils and prevent skin issues.




Siamese Cat


The Siamese cat is a breed of domestic cat that originated in Thailand (formerly known as Siam). It is one of the oldest and most recognized cat breeds in the world.


Siamese cats are known for their distinctive colorpoint coat, which means their body is lighter in color while their extremities (face, ears, paws, and tail) are darker.


There is also a more moderate and traditional version of the Siamese, known as the Thai cat, which resembles the original foundation stock with a rounder head and body.


They are known for their playful and social nature, as well as their vocal and demanding personalities.


Siamese cats are recognized by various cat fancier and breeder organizations worldwide and are bred by dedicated breeders who maintain their pedigrees.




British Shorthair


One of the most popular domestic cat in Hong Kong.


The British Shorthair is recognized as one of the oldest cat breeds, and to this day,
it continues to hold the title of the most popular pedigreed breed in its country of origin,
as confirmed by the UK's Governing Council of the Cat Fancy (GCCF).


It is characterized by its robust appearance, with a broad chest, muscular legs, rounded paws, and a medium-length tail with a blunt tip.


John Tenniel's renowned illustration of the Cheshire Cat in Alice in Wonderland drew inspiration from the British Shorthair breed.




Dragon Li (Chinese Li Hua)


The name Chinese Li Hua was developed from a common landrace of cats in China called 貍花貓 (líhuā māo), which means "leopard cat patterned cat" in English.


Characterized by its golden-brown, broken-mackerel tabby pattern, distinctive ear tipping, large round almond-shaped luminescent yellow/green eyes, and a strong, full-bodied stature reminiscent of its wild nature.


The Dragon Li remains a relatively rare breed.




Persian Cat


One of the oldeset, most popular and recognized cat around the world.


The Persian cat, also referred to as the Persian Longhair, is a breed known for its long, flowing coat and distinctive features like a round face and short muzzle.


Persian cats have a distinct facial structure with a round head, large round eyes, and a short, broad nose.


Due to their long hair, Persian cats are generally not as active or energetic as some other breeds. They prefer a relaxed and laid-back lifestyle.

Pallas's Cat


The BODY is ROUND!


The Pallas's cat has a round head, short legs, and a relatively short tail. Its fur is long, dense (most densest in feline), and usually grayish or ochre in color, with dark stripes and spots.


Pallas's cats have a distinct facial appearance with high-set eyes that have round pupils. They possess small, rounded ears and prominent facial markings, giving them an expression that is often described as "grumpy".


They inhabit the grasslands, montane steppes, and rocky slopes of Central Asia. They are found in countries like Mongolia, China, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, and Russia.


Pallas's cats are solitary and elusive animals. They are primarily active during dawn and dusk. They are skilled climbers and can navigate rocky terrains with ease.




Tiger


Tigers belongs to the Panthera genus in the Felidae family. It is one of the most iconic and recognizable big cats in the world.


They are known for their striking appearance, featuring a distinctive pattern of dark vertical stripes on a reddish-orange fur coat.


Native to various parts of Asia, including countries like India, Russia, China, and Indonesia.
They inhabit a range of diverse habitats such as tropical rainforests, mangrove swamps, grasslands, and even snowy regions.


Tigers have a significant cultural and symbolic value in many societies, often representing power, strength, and beauty across different cultures.




Snow Leopard


Snow leopard is native to the mountain ranges of Central and South Asia.


They are listed as Vulnerable on the IUCN Red List due to its small population of fewer than 10,000 mature individuals and a projected 10% decline by 2040.


Poaching and habitat destruction from infrastructure development are major threats to the species.


Snow leopard has a whitish to grey fur coat adorned with black spots on its head and neck, and bushy tail. It has a short muzzle, a domed forehead, and large nasal cavities.


The snow leopard's vocalizations include meowing, grunting, prusten and moaning. They can purr when exhaling.




Sand Cat


Sand cats are a small, solitary cat native to deserts in Africa and Asia. Superbly adapted to life in the desert, they can live without water, run on shifting sand and detect prey underground.


They have a sandy to grey-brown coat, with occasional leg stripes. They have red streaks on each cheek, a broad head with large eyes, low-set ears, and short limbs.


Sand cats prefer a very dry, arid habitat with little vegetation, for which they are well adapted.
They are sand-dwelling creatures, inhabiting dry plains and rocky valleys where conditions are extreme.




Serval


The Serval is a wild cat native to Africa.


It is a medium-sized cat with a slender body, large ears, and a golden-yellow to buff coat with black spots and stripes.


The Serval is a solitary carnivore that preys on rodents, birds, frogs, insects, and reptiles.


It is active both during the day and at night. The species is found in sub-Saharan countries, excluding rainforest regions, and is protected in many areas.


Servals have been associated with human beings since ancient times, particularly during Ancient Egypt. Art from that era portrays servals as gifts or commodities traded from Nubia.
